Digital Archeology

We arrived at CERN and spent the first day figuring out the task at hand: How do we pull apart the old WorldWideWeb browser to best emulate it in a contemporary browser?

Thankfully, we had an old NeXT box (almost but not quite the very one on which Tim Berners-Lee developed the WorldWideWeb application).

Oral Histories

We had a lovely lunch with Robert Cailliau, designer of the WWW logo (which we're using as our favicon!), and collaborator with Tim Berners-Lee on developing the World Wide Web.

Past Developers

Jean-Francois Groff hung out with us for a few days to help elucidate some of the design details of the WorldWideWeb browser, and to help us get some of the history straight. He wrote wwwlib with Tim Berners-Lee which became the core library for most modern web browsers.
